Who We Are

Kaylee Kimbrough

Kaylee Kimbrough is a 16-year-old high school junior from Dallas, Texas. She spent this summer in Round Rock, Texas participating in 7 weeks of intensive instruction in robotics, web development and design, mobile development, and mentoring from female engineers and entrepreneurs as a student in the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program. She has written for The Dallas Morning News and MissHeard Magazine. In the future she hopes to study transportation engineering and computer science.

Cristina M. Contreras

Cristina M. Contreras is a high school junior student from Colegio Puertorriqueño de Niñas in San Juan, Puerto Rico. She spend this summer in the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program where she met Kaylee and Emily; they decided to develop the A2B game. In her school Cristina is a member of various clubs and organizations. She is currently the president of the Environmental Science Organization and the secretary of her class. This year, she and a friend are starting a school newspaper.

Emily Chang

Emily Chang is a high school senior from Austin, Texas. She participated in the 7 week Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program, where she learned coding, robotics, and web development and formed friendships with like-minded girls. She is also the Executive Editor of her school’s student press, the Experience Design co-lead for the TEDxYouth@Austin team, and the Chief Marketing Officer of connect(ed) Digital, a non-profit organization dedicated to teaching elementary school students the basics of coding.
